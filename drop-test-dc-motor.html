How is it Done?

The drop test comes into play when suspected faults within the DC armature winding need careful assessment. Here's how it's done:

A direct current source is linked to the commutator segments. Introduce a variable resistance in the circuit's series and activate the DC supply. A milli-voltmeter is then linked to nearby segments. Using a variable rheostat, readings are adjusted to a specific value. By rotating the armature in a single direction, milli-voltmeter readings on the consecutive commutator segments are recorded.

The results are interpreted as:

Uniformity Indicator: If all readings match, the winding is correct. Short and Low: A millimeter reading of 0 or low voltage signals a short in the connected coil. High Voltage Alert: Indicate an open connection to the segments. Reverse Revelation: If the voltmeter shows a deflection in the opposite direction, it indicates that the coil linked to the segments has been reversed.
