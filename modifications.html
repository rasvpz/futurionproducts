Installation

The environment impacts the kind of motor that needs to be installed. All Variable Frequency Drive controlled motors must be rated by IEC or NEMA inverter duty. Article 430 of NEC must be complied with. Article 430 of NEC must be complied with. If the surface temperature exceeds 60 C, the motor needs to be provided with guarding.

Clear signs and arrows should be propped up so that no reverse-rotation scenarios occur, causing health hazard or equipment damage. RTDs and a temperature relay system are necessary for motors, so they shut down if very high temperatures are reached. There are certain performance characteristics that need to be adhered to with an S1 duty rating. A wire made of stranded copper must be ground insulated in the motor, with one end in the control panel that has the motor starter. Connection of the motor through wire nuts is prohibited except for certain exceptions. Boxes that contain the motor junction are supposed to be made of metal.
